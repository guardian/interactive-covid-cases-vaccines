This is Israel’s 2021 pandemic curve

Throughout 2021, Israel has managed to roll out its vaccine at impressive speed and show the world that a country can vaccinate its way out of the pandemic.

Peak comes in week seven

This curve shows how Israel’s Covid-19 deaths increased until the seventh week of the year. But this also shows the week in which its weekly vaccines peaks, before falling again as the vaccines took hold.

Deaths begin to fall as vaccines are rolled out

Weekly vaccines remained over 10,000 per million for several weeks as the country sough high vaccine coverage. It began to pay off from the eighth week, as deaths started to fall dramatically

This is the curve that other countries hope to copy

This arrow indicates how Israel’s deaths increased until higher vaccine coverage helped push its curve back to the left-hand side of this chart. It is a pattern that other countries hope to emulate.

Other countries are now seeing a similar pattern

Although death rates continued growing since the start of their own vaccination campaigns...

deaths in UK and US have fallen back dramatically after increasing their vaccinations.

And the same can be seen in wealthy European countries

Despite slow starts to their own vaccination campaigns, the same trend is now seen across major European economies; Spain, Italy, Germany and France. Deaths are down across the board - even as these countries are vaccinating more people than ever before.

But developing countries aren’t showing the same patterns

In a worrying sign that the unequal spread of vaccines around the world is creating a two-tier pandemic in which poorer countries are left behind, many developing countries are not following the same inverse C pattern of developed countries...

while each of Brazil, Argentina, India, Pakistan and South Africa are slowly increasing their vaccine rollout, each have yet to see a sustained decrease in the number of Covid-19 deaths they’re facing.
