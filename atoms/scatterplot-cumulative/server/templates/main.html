This is Israel’s 2021 pandemic curve

Throughout 2021, Israel has managed to roll out the vaccine at impressive speed. It's shown the world that a country can vaccinate its way out of the pandemic.

Israel’s peak came in 2021’s seventh week

This curve shows how Israel’s Covid-19 deaths increased until the seventh week of the year. At this point, just as its vaccination rollout was at its quickest pace, deaths started to fall.

Deaths began to fall as vaccines were rolled out

Weekly vaccines remained over 10,000 per million for several weeks as the country sought high vaccine coverage. It began to pay off from the eighth week, as deaths started to fall dramatically.

This is the curve that other countries hope to copy

This arrow indicates how Israel’s deaths increased until higher vaccine coverage helped push its curve back to the left-hand side of this chart. It is a pattern that other countries hope to emulate.

Other countries are now seeing a similar pattern

Death rates surged at the start of the year in both the UK and the US, just as the two developed countries began to ramp up their vaccination rollouts.

But now, with wider vaccination coverage, deaths have been pushed back to lower levels in both countries. They’ve succeeded in following Israel in having high vaccine numbers alongside low deaths

And the same can now be seen in wealthy European countries

Despite slow starts to their own vaccination campaigns, the same pattern can now be seen in major European economies such as Spain, Italy, Germany and France

Deaths are down across the board as these countries now have the majority of their vulnerable population protected by the vaccine.

But developing countries aren’t showing the same patterns

In a worrying sign that the unequal spread of vaccines around the world is creating a two-tier pandemic in which poorer countries are left behind, countries such as each of Brazil, Argentina, India, Peru and South Africa are not following the same inverse C pattern of developed countries.

After a slow first quarter for vaccine rollout, these developing countries are slowly increasing their vaccine distribution, but each has yet to see a sustained decrease in the number of Covid-19 deaths they’re facing.
